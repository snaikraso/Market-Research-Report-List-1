<p><p>The air heating sensor market includes two main types: Negative Temperature Coefficient (NTC) sensors and Positive Temperature Coefficient (PTC) sensors. NTC sensors decrease their resistance with increasing temperature, making them suitable for precise temperature monitoring and control in HVAC systems. In contrast, PTC sensors increase resistance as temperature rises, providing self-regulating heating capabilities, often used in anti-freezing applications and overcurrent protection. Both sensor types play vital roles in enhancing energy efficiency and optimizing temperature management in various environments.</p></p>
